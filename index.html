My Story

Since I was a kid, I have always wanted to build things. Whether it was a fort in the woods with my grandfather or a LEGO spaceship, I have always known and coveted that feeling of being able to step back, take a look at something, and say: "I made that."

As I got older, my drive to create found its medium in songs and stories, and with it I built recordings and novels.

Even as I moved into retail work, what I made with my own hands was what made me feel fulfilled - the clean counters and organized coolers; the oranges and apples stacked just so; the filled shelves and detailed planograms.

Aspirations

While working at a high-end grocery store as a produce buyer, I was frequently frustrated by the antiquated, manual pen and paper system used to order product. We didn't usually make margin as a department, and this was generally accepted by upper management as attributable to the notoriously difficult task of buying such a shrinkable product as fresh produce. I thought there had to be a better way. Having never been interested in programming in the past, I bought a book about the C language. I created a simple program in C that took our data based on past sales, our predictions for future sales, accounted our shrink and budget, and would effectively tell us what to order for the next day. We finally began making margin with regularity. Although I ended up replicating the algorithms in Excel, I had gotten my first taste in the creative power of programming.

If you told a younger me that I would be interested in programming, younger me would be astounded. But after taking Front End Web Development and SQL/ETL with Albany Can Code, I've realized the creative power of code to provide solutions that are not only satisfying to clients or end-users, but to the programmers themselves.

I hope to hone and expand my coding knowledge and combine it with my creative strengths in a workplace that allows me to look at a project, step back, and again say: "I made that."
